Fake/demo transcript. No real participants, clients, or confidential details.

Meeting title: AI Meeting Notes Training  
Department: Operations Enablement  
Meeting type: training  
Language hint: English  
Audience: team leads and coordinators  
Duration: 45 minutes  
Purpose: Practice turning a rough meeting transcript into a structured follow-up package using AI.

## Transcript

Facilitator: Today we are practicing how to use AI for meeting notes without uploading confidential data. For this demo, everything is fake. There are no real participants, no real clients, and no confidential information. The goal is to take rough notes or a rough transcript and turn them into a clean summary, action list, FAQ, risk log, and internal follow-up message.

Ops Lead: The main workflow we want to improve is post-training follow-up. Right now, people attend a session, ask useful questions, and then next steps disappear into chat threads. Sometimes the trainer sends a recap, sometimes the coordinator sends reminders, and sometimes nothing gets sent at all. We need something repeatable.

Coordinator A: I like the idea, but I am worried people will assume AI replaces the trainer or the coordinator. We should explain clearly that the tool drafts follow-up content and organizes information, but the trainer still reviews it before anything is shared.

Facilitator: Good concern. The ambassador message should say that AI is used for drafting and organizing, not for deciding policy, evaluating employees, or approving actions. A human reviewer remains responsible for checking accuracy, tone, privacy, and whether the message is appropriate for the audience.

Ops Lead: Exactly. We are not trying to automate judgment. We are trying to reduce admin work after trainings. If the AI can prepare a first draft of the summary, suggested actions, unanswered questions, and an FAQ update, that saves the trainer time and makes follow-up more consistent.

Coordinator B: I think the consistency part matters. After some trainings, employees get a really detailed recap. After others, they only get a short chat message. That creates confusion because teams start expecting different levels of follow-up depending on who hosted the session.

Facilitator: For the MVP, we want a simple process. A coordinator pastes a fake or approved transcript into the AI tool. The tool produces a structured follow-up package. Then the reviewer checks it in Google Sheets before anything is sent. We are keeping the workflow simple so teams actually use it.

Coordinator A: Are we saying Google Sheets is the official review layer for now?

Facilitator: Yes, for the MVP. We decided to keep Google Sheets as the review layer because everyone already understands it. It is not perfect, but it is familiar. We did not decide whether the approved message should go out by email, Telegram, or both.

Ops Lead: I prefer email for anything formal because it creates a clearer record. Telegram is faster, but important information can get buried quickly. Maybe the formal summary goes by email and a short reminder goes in Telegram.

Coordinator B: That could work, but we should not decide today unless the communications team agrees. Some departments use Telegram heavily, while others barely use it.

Facilitator: Good point. Let’s mark the distribution channel as unresolved. The options are email only, Telegram only, or email plus a short Telegram notification. Communications should advise before we finalize that.

Ops Lead: Sarah will prepare a one-page reviewer checklist by Friday. The checklist should tell reviewers what to check before approving AI-generated notes. It should include accuracy, missing action owners, sensitive information, tone, and whether the final message matches company policy.

Coordinator A: Should the checklist also include a reminder to remove names if they are not needed?

Facilitator: Yes. The checklist should say that names should only be included when necessary for action ownership or accountability. Otherwise, summaries should use roles or teams where possible.

Coordinator B: Ravi will collect common employee questions from the next two demo sessions. We did not agree on a deadline yet, but the purpose is to build a starter FAQ. Facilitator: Good addition. The FAQ entry should say: do not paste customer data, employee personal information, HR or personnel issues, legal matters, vendor contracts, confidential strategy notes, non-public financial details, or anything that has not been approved for AI processing.

Instead of starting from a blank page every time, the trainer gets a structured draft.
